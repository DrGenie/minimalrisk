Minimal Risks Decision Aid Tool for MS Treatments in Pragmatic Clinical Trials (PCTs)

Background

This tool is based on a simulated discrete choice experiment (DCE) survey with 400 patients with multiple sclerosis (MS). The DCE used a Bayesian D-efficient design to generate choice tasks involving two hypothetical treatment alternatives and a status quo option (risks if not participating in the PCT). The risks considered were selected by an advisory committee as important to patients.

Risks attributes:

  • Serious Infection Risk: Low (1%), Medium (3%), High (5%)
  • Neurological Side Effects Risk: Low (0.5%), Medium (2%), High (4%)
  • Hospitalization Risk per Year: Low (0.1), Medium (0.3), High (0.5)

A multinomial logit model was estimated to derive preference weights. The status quo represents the risks of the current treatment or no participation.

Sample size determined using power analysis methods (DOI: 10.1007/s40271-015-0118-z).
