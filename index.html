Minimal Risks Decision Aid Tool for MS Treatments in Pragmatic Clinical Trials (PCTs)

Background

This tool is based on a discrete choice experiment (DCE) from a study evaluating patient preferences for relapsing-remitting multiple sclerosis (RRMS) treatments (DOI: 10.2147/PPA.S306498). The DCE involved 150 patients choosing between hypothetical treatments with varying risk levels. A hierarchical Bayesian model was used to derive preference weights. Relative importance values were used to calculate betas for risk attributes, assuming linear coding based on percentage levels.

Risk attributes and levels (derived from the study):

  • Risk of gastrointestinal symptoms: <1% (0.5%), 10%, 25%
  • Risk of flu-like symptoms: <1% (0.5%), 30%, 70%
  • Risk of infection: 20%, 45%, 80%
  • Risk of life-threatening event: 0%, 0.01%, 0.1%

The status quo represents the risks if not participating in the PCT (utility = 0). Sample size aligns with power analysis methods (DOI: 10.1007/s40271-015-0118-z).
